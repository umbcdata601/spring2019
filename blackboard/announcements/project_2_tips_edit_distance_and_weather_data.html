The first question was on merging data. When the fields to be merged are not exact matches, the merge fails. This may be due to misspelling of the entries or different use of white space ("a key" versus "a   key") or a difference in case (my_key versus My_Key). The white space can be addressed using a regex and the case difference can be resolved using the .lower() method. The situation where words may have different spellings is trickier. The concept of edit distance is how many changes are needed to convert one string to another. 

For a Python-based explanation of edit distance, see https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/edit-distance-dp-5/ and https://www.python-course.eu/levenshtein_distance.php
There's a command available in NLTK https://www.nltk.org/api/nltk.metrics.html#nltk.metrics.distance.edit_distance
and there are example implementations https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Algorithm_Implementation/Strings/Levenshtein_distance#Python
